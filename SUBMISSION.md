# MUSE∞ submission package

## One-line description

MUSE∞ is an AI-native cultural world where artists and thinkers disagree, the visitor chooses a philosophy, and that choice physically rewrites the museum around them.

## What makes it different

Most AI museum experiences place a chatbot beside an artwork. MUSE∞ uses dialogue as a world-control system. Every response maps to a constrained spatial effect, and the visitor’s final decision changes the environment, philosophical state, ending, and manifesto.

## Two-minute pitch

Museums are constrained by geography, physical ownership, and one-directional interpretation. But people do not only want more information about art. They want to enter a perspective, ask questions, and form an interpretation of their own.

MUSE∞ is an AI-native spatial museum where visitors move through artistic worlds and summon an impossible salon across centuries. The core loop is simple: ask a question, hear genuine disagreement, choose a perspective, and watch that choice become architecture.

The current prototype demonstrates the entire reliable loop without depending on external services. A deterministic state machine controls the journey. Structured dialogue maps to a limited visual-effect vocabulary. Philosophy scoring selects a personalized ending. A particle renderer turns perception, emotion, and invention into visible spatial change.

The next integration replaces cached dialogue with GPT-5.6 structured output while preserving the same deterministic effects and fallback path. Codex was used to translate the creative direction into the working architecture, implement the main experience, and validate it end-to-end in the browser.

We are not digitizing museums. We are creating the museum that physical reality could never contain.
