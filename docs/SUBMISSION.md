# MUSE∞ submission package

## One-line description

MUSE∞ is an AI-native dream museum where one personal question becomes a walkable 3D gallery of open-access artworks, GPT-5.6 companions, and living world transformations.

## What makes it different

Most AI museum experiences place a chatbot beside an artwork. MUSE∞ turns the visitor's own question into the curatorial engine. GPT-5.6 builds a thematic journey, companions respond to real artwork metadata, and every response maps to constrained spatial effects that visibly change the gallery.

## Two-minute pitch

Museums are constrained by geography, physical ownership, and one-directional interpretation. But people do not only want more information about art. They want to bring a personal question into culture and see how different works, artists, and ideas change the way they understand it.

MUSE∞ is an AI-native dream museum where one question becomes a continuous walkable gallery. The visitor asks something personal, chooses a few artist and thinker companions, and moves through connected regions filled with open-access artworks selected around that theme.

The current prototype demonstrates the reliable loop without depending on external services. A deterministic state machine controls the journey. Open-access museum records load through a server proxy. Structured GPT-5.6 dialogue maps to a limited visual-effect vocabulary. Philosophy scoring selects a personalized ending. The renderer turns interpretation into visible spatial change.

The next integration deepens the curatorial journey: GPT-5.6 should generate the question-specific gallery plan, World Labs should generate a connected base environment, and Tripo should produce reviewed 3D companion figures from approved multiview references. All paid generation remains server-only and opt-in, so the judging path is still runnable without credentials.

We are not digitizing museums. We are creating a museum that begins with the visitor's question and becomes a world around it.
